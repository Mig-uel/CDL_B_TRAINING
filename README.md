# CDL B Training

## Pre-Trip Inspection

The pre-trip inspection is a critical component of CDL B road test. It involves a thorough examination of the vehicle to ensure it is safe and in proper working condition before operating it on the road. The inspection is divided into two forms, Form A and Form B, which cover different areas of the vehicle. In the road test for CDL B, you will either be required to perform Form A or Form B, rarely both, and you will also be required to perform in-cab inspection and brake tests.

**Form A**

- Front of vehicle
- Engine compartment
- Steering
- Front axle

**Form B**

- Fuel area
- Under the vehicle
- Rear Axle
- Suspension
- Rear of the vehicle

**In-Cab Inspection and Brake Tests**

- Interior of the vehicle
- Air brake tests
  - Air Leakage Test
  - Low Air Pressure Signal Test
  - Automatic Spring Brake Test
  - Parking Brake Test
  - Service Brake Test

## Helpful Terminology

- **All Parts Are** - **Properly, Mounted, and Secure** (**PMS**)
- **All Parts Are Not** - **Cracked, Bent, or Broken** (**CBB**)
- **All Hoses Have No** - **Abrasions, Bulges, or Cuts** (**ABC**)
- **Gauges and Fluids Are Within** - **Safe Operating Range** (**SOR**) and **Above Refill Mark** (**ARM**)

### General Tips

- If it is mounted (almost everything is) say it is **Properly Mounted and Secure**.
- It it is metal or some other hard surface, say it is **Not Cracked, Bent, or Broken**.
- If it is rubber, say it has **No Abrasions, Bulges, or Cuts**.
- If it contains fluids, say the levels are **Within Safe Operating Range** and **Above Refill Mark**.
- For fluids, say **there are no visible leaks**.
- For air parts, say **there are no audible leaks**.
- For tires (**I-D-C**), **Inflation, Depth, and Condition**.
- **DOG** - **Debris, Oil, and Grease**
- For **Form A Front Suspension** remember **BLUSH** - **Bushings, Leaf Springs, U-Bolts, Shock Absorbers, and Hangers**.
- For **Form B Rear Suspension** remember **LBS** - **Leaf Springs, Bushings, and Shock Absorbers**.

## Road Test Setup

- Turn on the **Kill Switch** located on the passenger side of the vehicle.
- Enter the vehicle maintaining three points of contact and check that the parking brake is set.
- Adjust the seat, put on the seatbelt, and steering wheel to your preference.
- Safe start (turn the key to the first click and continue to the second click to start the engine).
- Make sure all necessary lights are on (High and Low Beams, Clearance Lights, and 4-way flashers).
- Shut off the engine, remove the key and put it in your pocket, and exit the vehicle maintaining three points of contact.
- Go where the examiner instructs you to perform either Form A or Form B

## Form A

- Perform the road test setup.
  - Turn on the **Kill Switch** located on the passenger side of the vehicle.
  - Enter the vehicle maintaining three points of contact and check that the parking brake is set.
  - Adjust the seat, put on the seatbelt, and steering wheel to your preference.
  - Safe start (turn the key to the first click and continue to the second click to start the engine).
  - Make sure all necessary lights are on (High and Low Beams, Clearance Lights, and 4-way flashers).
  - Shut off the engine, remove the key and put it in your pocket, and exit the vehicle maintaining three points of contact.

- Perform Light Check
  - "The 4-way flashers, clearance lights, high and low beams, left and right turn signals are all **Properly Mounted and Secure** and are **Not Cracked, Bent, or Broken**.
